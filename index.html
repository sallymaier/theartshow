about

This whole thing is our producer Kent Renyolds's fault. He came to me with the idea of creating a long format, video podcast about the arts. It'll be professional he said. It's the future of entertainment he said. We can make art talks interesting and expand the art community.

How could i say no to that!

What we have here are exciting conversations about art, punctuated by the ramblings and diversions of a sculptor pretending to be a tv host. In just two weeks, from the headquarters of the Washington Project for the Arts, we created over 16 hours of art conversation with 21 guests, smashed some mugs, got a tattoo on air, and began the process of creating a lasting place for art conversation and critique. Its not perfect, gets a little awkward and slow at times, but that's what fast forward is for. Other parts are truly innovative, insightful, and inspiring and I thank the guests for making it possible. It truly is the talent of all of our guests that made this a solid platform to build on. We will be filming more episodes once a week and releasing them as they are made. As long as you the viewer keep watching, and if Kent doesn't murder me in the process, we hope to continue to create sleeker and more innovation art conversations to share the people and ideas that excite and inspire us with you.

Thanks to the Washington Project for the Arts and its wonderful staff for allowing us to film our first season in their space and for stickiing around late into the evening as we recorded.

Thanks to Rania Hassan for her help making our fantastic logo.

Thanks to Sally Maier for realizing Kent and my limitations as graphic designers and building us this amazing website.

Especially thanks to Kent Reynolds for his tireless work and devotion to this project, supplying the equipment, learning how to record and edit, and generally doing everything single handed to make this possible. You are a beautiful boy and I appreciate you.

-calder
